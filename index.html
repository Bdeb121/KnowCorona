About COVID-19


What is COVID-19 (Corona-Virus)

COVID-19 is a pandemic which is caused by SARS-CoV2 , it belongs to a large virus family causing illness ranging from common cold to other several diseases. These viruses are called Corona viruses, this disease started in Wuhan city of china and then started spreading all over the world , Based on the transmission rate on 11th Feb 2020, World Health Organization announced COVID-19 as Global Health Emergency.

As this disease started in December 2019 and the group of virus in SARS (Severe Acute Respiratory Syndrome) and the Strain is 2 hence new virus is named as SARS-CoV2 Severe Acute Respiratory Syndrome Corona-virus 2 by I.C.T.V. International Committee on Taxonomy of Viruses.


What are Corona Viruses

The name "coronavirus" is derived from Latin corona, meaning "crown" or "wreath". Corona viruses are a family of positive single stranded RNA viruses.
Classified under ....
Realm -- Riboviria
Phylum -- Incertae sedis
Order -- Nidovirales
Family -- Coronaviridae
Sub-family -- Orthocoronavirinae

They have a Nucleo-Capsid of helical symmetry, the genome size range from approximately 26 to 32 kb (kilo-bases) & 80 to 120 nanometer in diameter, one of the largest among RNA viruses. They have characterize club shaped spike that protect from their surface, which is electron micrographs created an image reminiscent of solar corona.

History & Reproduction of COVID-19

History of Corona Virus

Corona viruses were first discovered in 1930s , When on Acute Respiratory infection of domesticated chickens was shown to be caused by Infectious Bronchitis(IBV). Arthus Schalk and M.C Hawn described in 1931 a new respiratory infection of chickens in North Dakota.

Human corona viruses were discovered in 1960s. E.C Kendall , Malcolm Byone & David Tyrell isolated a novel common cold Virus B814 from a boy, The Coronavirus B814 which was infecting humans , lost after some decades , other human corona virus have since been identified includes, SARS-coV in 2003 , HcoV NL63 in 2004 , HcoV HKU1 in 2005 , MERS-coV in 2012 & SARS-coV2 in 2019.


Manner of Development of Disease

1  The virus first bind to the cell surface receptors through s-protein , followed by fusion of viruses envelope to the cell membrane.

2  Following the fusion, the RNA released into the host cell.

3  The first step include genome RNA translation into viral replicase polyproteins pp1a and 1ab.

4  After transcription and translation the viral proteins and genome RNA form an assembly in Endoplasmic reticulum and Golgi complex and in finally released out of the cell by ExoCytosis.

5  The virus particle have an affinity to infect the lungs, through the blood stream.

6  They also involve liver and kidney.

Transmission & Treatment of COVID-19

How corona virus spread

NOTE :- There is no proven study so far which suggest that corona virus can spread through air.

 1  Droplet Transmission.....

Transmission occurs when you have close contact (within one metre) with a person who has respiratory symptoms such as coughing or sneezing, which may spread these potentially infectious droplets, typically 5-10 microns in size, to your body.

 2  By touching surfaces around infected person.....

 3  Mass gathering.....

when large no of people gather in a place than there will be a very high chances of skin contact , or droplet transmission & hence it will result into infection of disease.

Treatment of COVID-19

NOTE :- There’s currently no treatment specifically approved for COVID-19, its vaccine is under trial. We can only subside the symptoms of the patient by giving supportive care. Some of the therapies we can use are

 1  Antiviral or retroviral medications

 2  Breathing support, such as mechanical ventilation

 3  Steroids to reduce lung swelling

 4  Blood plasma transfusions etc...
